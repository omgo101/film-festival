Festival Dignitaries & Team

Hyderabad International Short Film Festival 2025

Festival Collaborators

Shri DIL RAJU

Producer • Chairman, Telangana Film Development Corporation (TFDC)

Award-winning Indian film producer & distributor (Sri Venkateswara Creations). Two National Awards (Best Popular Film) for Sathamanam Bhavati (2016) & Maharshi (2019); Nagi Reddy–Chakrapani Award (2013). Producer of Arya, Bommarillu, Fidaa, Balagam. Serving as TFDC Chairman since late-2024.

Smt. Priyanka, IAS

Managing Director, Telangana Film Development Corporation Limited (TGFDC)

Civil servant and arts advocate strengthening Telangana's creative industries — building infrastructure, incentives, and pathways for film, TV & theatre; positioning Telangana as a hub for cultural and cinematic growth.

Festival Promoters

Shri JAYESH RANJAN

Special Chief Secretary (FAC) – Youth Advancement

Tourism & Culture Department, Government of Telangana

Senior IAS officer leading Youth Advancement, Tourism & Culture. Drives initiatives across entrepreneurship, tourism, digital innovation and youth empowerment; shaping Telangana's forward-looking global image.

Smt. VALLURU KRANTHI, IAS

Managing Director – Telangana Tourism Development Corporation Limited

Director (FAC) – Department of Tourism, Government of Telangana

Administrator championing heritage, infrastructure, and experiential tourism. Leads sustainable, community-centric projects expanding Telangana's cultural tourism footprint.

Festival Patron & Advisors

C. Umamaheswara Rao

Festival Patron

National Award-winning director & screenwriter. Former Jury Chair (West) and Member (Central) at the 67th National Awards. Founder of Dadasaheb Phalke School of Film Studies; noted for films like Ankuram that explore human rights and relations.

PREMENDRA MAZUMDER

Festival Advisor

Film critic, curator, and organiser; VP, Federation of Film Societies of India; General Secretary, FIPRESCI India; founder of South Asian Short Film Festival & World Film Festival Kolkata. Recipient of Vijaya Mulay (2023), Satyajit Ray Memorial (2018), Ritwik Ghatak (2016).

Christopher Dalton

Festival Advisor

Film critic, journalist, and festival organiser. Founder, Cinema Society of India; CEO, House of Illusions. Co-author and editor (Cinematic Illusions; Journal of Indian Cinema). Served on the Jury of the 67th National Film Awards.

Also Supported By

Members of the Society of Dadasaheb Phalke School of Film Studies.

Administrative Team

NANDAN BABU

Festival Director

M.Sc. Electronic Media; 18+ years across film & media. Assistant Director & Cinematographer (under Madhu Ambat), ad films director, Programming Head (Radio Mirchi), Content Editor (Gaana South). Principal, Dadasaheb Phalke School of Film Studies (8+ years).

MADHU MAHANKALI

Festival Director

Madhu Mahankali, an FTII Pune alumnus, is a filmmaker with 15+ feature films across Assamese, Telugu, and Hindi. Through Dhriti Media Pvt. Ltd., he has produced numerous TV shows and ad films. His short The Journey and feature Parampara (2014) won international awards, and his works, including In Search of Buddha, have been showcased at global film festivals.

HESHVANTH GURUKUL

Festival Operations Head

Assistant Director on Telugu features; writing team with director Bommarillu Bhaskar. Short film Breaking Out screened nationally & internationally. Leads HISFF operations, fostering cinematic excellence and dialogue.

NASSER

Actor • Director • President, Nadigar Sangam

Acclaimed actor-filmmaker with landmark roles (Roja, Bombay, Baahubali). Strong advocate of artists' welfare; multiple honours across states and national forums.

NAGESH KUKUNOOR

Writer • Director

Pioneer of Indian new-wave cinema with Hyderabad Blues, Iqbal, Dor. National & international awards; mentor to emerging filmmakers.

M. SASHI KUMAR

Founder, Asianet • Chairman, Asiaville Interactive

Journalist, media entrepreneur, filmmaker; Founder & Chair, Media Development Foundation (Asian College of Journalism). Pioneered India's first regional satellite channel; strong voice on journalism ethics and education.

SUNZU BACHASPATIMAYUM

Documentary Filmmaker

Radicalised Manipuri documentary cinema with Rude Awakening. Producer of National Award-winning AFSPA 1958; Secretary, MSFDS; Director, Eikhoigi Imphal IFF.

ALEXANDER LEO POU

Writer • Director • Producer

SRFTI alumnus; former Director, MSFTI; Film Facilitation Officer (East), NFDC Kolkata; coordinated national language-documentation project; Joint Director, EIIFF (2025).

MANJU BORAH

Writer • Director

Eminent filmmaker from Assam/Northeast; recipient of nine National Awards. Works include Baibhab, Ko Yad, and animated feature on Srimanta Sankardev; strong cultural & academic contributor.
